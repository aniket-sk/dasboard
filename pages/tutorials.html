Graph of open and close
  • Graph of open and close is a bar chart that shows open and closing prices of a stock for a given period of time, with the closing price being considered the most important by many traders.
  • The closing price is the raw price or cash value of the last transacted price in a security before the market officially closes for normal trading. It is often the reference point used by investors to compare a stock's performance since the previous dayâ€”and closing prices are frequently used to construct line graphs depicting historical price changes over time.
  • The chart type is useful because it can show increasing or decreasing momentum. When the open and close are far apart it shows strong momentum, and when the open and close are close together it shows indecision or weak momentum. The high and low show the full price range of the period, useful in assessing volatility.

Graph of volume
  • A price by volume (PBV) graph is a graph which shows the volume of shares traded at a specific price level.
  • Price by volume charts are used to illustrate high buying and selling interest at specific price levels.
  • They are indicative of price levels over a certain period of time.
  • They are generally used in conjunction with other forms of technical analysis.
